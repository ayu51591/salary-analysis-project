# Salary Analysis in Data Careers

## Objective
To analyze how job role, experience, and industry influence salary in data-related careers.

---

## Dataset
The dataset includes:
- Job roles (Data Analyst, Data Scientist, etc.)
- Experience (years)
- Industry
- Salary

---

## Methodology
The analysis was performed in stages:
1. Explored dataset structure and key variables  
2. Compared salary across job roles  
3. Analyzed salary growth with experience  
4. Evaluated industry-wise salary and job distribution  
5. Compared impact of role vs industry  

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Role Impact
Job role has the strongest impact on salary.  
Data Scientists consistently earn more than Data Analysts.

---

### 2. Experience Impact
Salary increases with experience initially.  
However, Data Analyst salaries tend to plateau at higher experience levels, while Data Scientist salaries continue to grow.

---

### 3. Industry Impact
Industry affects salary slightly, but the variation is small compared to role-based differences.

---

### 4. Job Opportunities
Industries like Technology and Consulting offer more job opportunities along with competitive salaries.

---

### 5. Role vs Industry
The salary difference between roles (~15–20k) is significantly higher than the differences between industries (~2–4k).  
This shows that role specialisation is more important than industry selection.

---

## Conclusion
Students aiming for long-term salary growth should focus on role specialization, particularly moving toward advanced roles like Data Scientist.  
Industry selection is important for job opportunities, but has a limited impact on salary compared to job role.

---

## Limitations
- The dataset may contain synthetic values  
- No time-based trends  
- Limited variables considered
